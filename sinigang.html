Sinigang

Ingredients needed

- Two pounds. belly of pork

- One pound. young tamarind

- One bunch of sliced water spinach

- Eight string beans, each chopped into 2-inch chunks

- Two cut eggplants

- One daikon radish piece

- Eight chunks of okra

- Two tomatoes, cut into wedges

- Two long green pepper slices

- One onion, cut into wedges

- Two quarts of water

- Ground black pepper with fish sauce

Procedure:

- For forty minutes, bring two quarts of water to a boil with the young tamarind.

- Use a colander or a kitchen sieve to filter the tamarind broth. After that, squeeze the tamarind to release any leftover juices.

- Fill a cooking saucepan with the tamarind broth. Add the pork belly, onion, and half the tomatoes after letting it boil.

- After removing the floating scum, add 1 tablespoon of fish sauce, cover, and simmer for an additional hour.

- Add the eggplants and daikon radish. Cook for five minutes.

- Add the remaining tomatoes, okra, string beans, and long green pepper. Cook for three minutes.

- Add the remaining tomatoes, okra, string beans, and long green pepper. Cook for three minutes.

- After adding the chopped water spinach stalks, season with ground black pepper and fish sauce. Cook for two minutes.

- Add the water spinach leaves. Shut off the heat and cover. Before serving, let the leaves simmer for three minutes in the remaining heat.

- Enjoy and spread the word!
